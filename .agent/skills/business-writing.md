# SKILL: Business Writing

**Trigger:** When @biz needs to create professional business documents for external stakeholders — partners, investors, clients, or internal executive summaries.

---

## When to Use
- Writing a partnership proposal or pitch to a potential partner.
- Creating a case study from a successful feature or user story.
- Drafting an executive summary for stakeholders.
- Preparing B2B outreach materials.
- Any professional business communication beyond marketing content.

---

## Document Types & Templates

### 1. Partnership Proposal
```markdown
# Partnership Proposal: [Our Product] × [Partner Name]

## Executive Summary
[2-3 sentences: what we propose, why it benefits both parties]

## About [Our Product]
[1 paragraph: what we do, who we serve, key metrics]

## The Opportunity
[What specific problem does this partnership solve?]
- **For [Partner]:** [Their benefit — quantified]
- **For us:** [Our benefit — quantified]

## Proposed Collaboration
| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Type** | [Co-marketing / API integration / Distribution / Revenue share] |
| **Scope** | [What each party does] |
| **Timeline** | [Proposed phases] |
| **Success metrics** | [How we'll measure success] |

## Next Steps
1. [Specific action item]
2. [Specific action item]

## Contact
[Name] — [Email] — [Title]
```

### 2. Case Study
```markdown
# Case Study: [Title — Result-First]

## The Challenge
[What problem did the user/client face? Be specific.]

## The Solution
[How our product addressed it — features used, approach taken]

## The Results
| Metric | Before | After | Improvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Key metric] | [Value] | [Value] | [% change] |

## Key Takeaway
[One sentence — the lesson or value demonstrated]

## Quote
> "[Customer testimonial or user feedback]"
> — [Name, Title]
```

### 3. Executive Summary
```markdown
# Executive Summary: [Topic]
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD | **Author:** @biz

## Situation
[Current state — 2-3 sentences]

## Key Findings
1. **[Finding 1]:** [One-sentence explanation + data]
2. **[Finding 2]:** [One-sentence explanation + data]
3. **[Finding 3]:** [One-sentence explanation + data]

## Recommendation
[What we should do — 1-2 sentences]

## Impact
| Metric | Expected Change | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| [Metric] | [+/- X%] | [Timeframe] |

## Decision Required
[What decision the reader needs to make]
```

### 4. B2B Outreach Email
```markdown
Subject: [Partnership / Integration] Opportunity — [Our Product] × [Their Product]

Hi [Name],

[One sentence: why you're reaching out — reference their product specifically]

[One sentence: what you do and why it's relevant to them]

[One sentence: the specific opportunity — what could joint efforts look like?]

[One sentence: concrete next step — "Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?"]

Best,
[Name]
[Title] — [Product]
```

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone
- **Professional but human.** Not corporate-speak, not casual.
- **Data-first.** Every claim backed by a number or source.
- **Action-oriented.** Every document ends with a clear next step.
- **Audience-aware.** Technical details for technical partners; business impact for executives.

### Structure Rules
- Lead with the **benefit to the reader**, not about yourself.
- Use **bullet points and tables** over long paragraphs.
- Keep paragraphs to **3 sentences max**.
- Include **one clear CTA** per document.
- Use **bold** for key metrics and decision points.

### Common Mistakes
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "35M Vietnamese check lunar dates daily" | "We have a large addressable market" |
| "Integration would give your users X" | "We would love to partner with you" |
| "Users report 40% time savings" | "Our product is very efficient" |

## Rules
- **Never send externally without @pm approval.**
- **Source every claim.** Unsourced metrics are fiction.
- **Match recipient's language.** Vietnamese for local partners; English for international.
- **Follow up.** Every outreach gets a follow-up timeline.
