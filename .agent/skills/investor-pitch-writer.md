# SKILL: INVESTOR PITCH WRITER

**Trigger:** When the User needs to create a pitch one-pager, executive summary, or investor-facing document from existing project documentation.

---

## When to Use
- Preparing for angel investor meetings.
- Creating a one-pager to pitch a startup idea.
- Distilling technical PRD/architecture into business language.
- Running the `/prep-pitch-deck` workflow.

---

## Input Gathering

Before writing, scan and extract key data from:
1. **PRD / Product Brief** — problem, solution, features, metrics.
2. **Architecture / Codebase** — what's already built (traction proof).
3. **Research reports** — market size, competitive landscape.
4. **Existing marketing materials** — if any.

---

## One-Pager Structure

```markdown
# [Product Name]
*[One-line tagline: what it does, for whom]*

---

## The Problem
[2-3 sentences. What pain exists? Who feels it? How big is it?]
- [Stat or data point proving the problem is real]

## The Solution
[2-3 sentences. What does the product do? How is it different?]
- **Key differentiator:** [What makes this unique — the USP]

## Market Opportunity
- **TAM:** [Total Addressable Market with source]
- **SAM:** [Serviceable Available Market]
- **SOM:** [Serviceable Obtainable Market — realistic near-term]
- **Target segment:** [Primary user demographic]

## Traction & Progress
[What's already built? Any users, revenue, or validation?]
- [Milestone 1: e.g., "MVP launched with X features"]
- [Milestone 2: e.g., "Y monthly active users"]
- [Milestone 3: e.g., "Featured on Z platform"]

## Business Model
[How does the product make money?]
- **Revenue streams:** [Subscription / Freemium / Ads / Licensing]
- **Pricing:** [Price points or tiers]
- **Unit economics:** [CAC, LTV if available]

## Roadmap
| Phase | Timeline | Deliverable |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Current | [Now] | [What's live] |
| Next | [Q+1] | [What's coming] |
| Future | [Q+2-3] | [Vision] |

## The Team
[Brief team intro — relevant experience and domain expertise]

## The Ask
- **Raising:** [Amount]
- **Use of funds:** [How the money will be deployed]
- **Milestone this unlocks:** [What the funding enables]

## Contact
[Name] — [Email] — [LinkedIn/Website]
```

---

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone
- **Concise:** Max 1 page when printed. Every sentence earns its place.
- **Data-driven:** Replace vague claims with numbers. "Large market" → "₫50T market".
- **No jargon:** If your grandmother can't understand it, rewrite it. Technical terms → business impact.
- **Confident but honest:** State what's real. Don't exaggerate traction.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "[X users] rely on [product category] daily" | "Huge market opportunity" |
| "MVP live with 5 core features" | "Building a revolutionary platform" |
| "Revenue from premium tier at ₫49k/mo" | "Multiple monetization strategies" |
| "Solo founder, 10 years in software" | "World-class team" |

### Formatting
- Use **bold** for key metrics and differentiators.
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences max.
- Use bullet points over long prose.
- Include one compelling visual if possible (chart, product screenshot).

---

## Quality Checklist
Before delivering:
- [ ] Fits on one page (max ~500 words)
- [ ] Problem is backed by data, not assumptions
- [ ] USP is clear in one sentence
- [ ] Market size has at least TAM and SAM
- [ ] Traction section shows proof, not promises
- [ ] The Ask is specific (amount + use of funds)
- [ ] Zero technical jargon
- [ ] A non-technical person can understand it in 60 seconds
